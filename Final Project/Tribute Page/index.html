Irena Sendler

A Polish social worker

Here's a time line of Irena Sendler life:

  • February 15, 1910 - Born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1939 - With the outbreak of World War II, Sendler began aiding Jews in Nazi-occupied Warsaw by providing them with false documents and finding them safe hiding places.
  • 1940-1943 - Sendler joined Zegota, an underground organization dedicated to rescuing Jews. She became the head of its children's section and organized the smuggling of Jewish children out of the Warsaw Ghetto.
  • 1943 - Sendler was arrested by the Gestapo and imprisoned, but she refused to reveal any information about the children she had helped save. Despite being tortured, she never betrayed their identities or locations.
  • 1944 - Sendler narrowly escaped execution and continued her resistance activities until the end of the war.
  • Post War - Sendler worked as a social worker, dedicating her life to helping disadvantaged children and families.
  • 1965 - Sendler was recognized by Yad Vashem, Israel's Holocaust memorial, as one of the Righteous Among the Nations for her heroism during the Holocaust.
  • 2007 - Sendler passed away on May 12, 2008. Her extraordinary bravery and compassion continue to inspire people around the world.
  • 2008 - dies at the age of 95.
